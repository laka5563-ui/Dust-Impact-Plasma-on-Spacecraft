# Dust Impact Plasma Plume Magnetic Field Model

## Overview

This project computes the magnetic field generated by a plasma plume formed after a hypervelocity dust impact on a spacecraft.

The model simulates the motion of charged particles (ions and electrons) and calculates the resulting magnetic field at an arbitrary observation point using physical principles.

---

## Physics Background

When a dust particle impacts a spacecraft:

- A plasma cloud consisting of ions and electrons is generated
- Electrons move faster than ions due to their lower mass
- The motion of charged particles produces a transient magnetic field

The magnetic field is computed using the Biot–Savart law:

\[
B = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{q(\vec{v} \times \hat{r})}{R^2}
\]

where:
- \( q \) = particle charge  
- \( \vec{v} \) = particle velocity  
- \( R \) = distance between particle and sensor  

---

## Features Implemented

- Simulation of plasma plume using:
  - Ion population
- Velocity distribution (Gaussian)
- Directional emission (cosine distribution)
- Magnetic field computation using Biot–Savart law
- Magnetic field evaluated at an arbitrary sensor position

---

## Model Description

- The plasma plume is modeled as a collection of charged particles
- Each particle contributes to the magnetic field at the sensor
- The total magnetic field is obtained by summing contributions from all particles over time

---

## Outputs

The code computes:

- Time-varying magnetic field components:
  - Bx
  - By
  - Bz
- Magnetic field magnitude |B|

---

## Assumptions

- Plasma is collisionless after impact
- Particles expand freely from the impact point
- Magnetic field contributions are computed independently (no collective plasma effects)
- Spacecraft interaction effects are simplified

---

## Limitations

- Does not include full spacecraft charging dynamics
- No full Maxwell-equation-based electromagnetic simulation
- No inverse modeling or impact localization
- Simplified plasma expansion model
